How a Karachi Logistics Startup Found Its Gap in Two Weeks

Fourteen operators mapped, three underserved routes surfaced, and a scoped MVP — what the first two weeks of Dhundo actually produced.

Case Studies

Case Studies

The founder came to us with a conviction rather than a plan: Karachi's logistics market was broken, and there was a business in fixing it. Both things were true. Neither told us what to build.

Week one: map the operators

We catalogued fourteen operators running intra-city freight, and the routes each one actually served. Not the routes on their marketing sites — the routes their dispatchers confirmed when we called as customers.

The map made the gap visible almost immediately. Three corridors had demand and effectively no reliable coverage, because they were awkward enough to schedule that the big operators declined them.

Week two: size it and scope it

An underserved route is only an opportunity if someone will pay for it to be served. We spoke to shippers on those three corridors and found consistent willingness to pay a premium for reliability they weren't getting.

That turned into a scoped MVP: a driver app and a dispatch console, aimed only at those corridors, with everything else deliberately left out.

What made it work

Nothing here was clever. It was a fortnight of phone calls and a spreadsheet. The value wasn't insight nobody could have had — it was doing the unglamorous work before committing a year of build to a guess.
